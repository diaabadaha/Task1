Main Contact Meals
  • Mansaf

    Mansaf is a traditional Jordanian and Palestinian dish that is often considered the national dish of Jordan. It is also popular in other Middle Eastern countries. Mansaf is a festive and culturally significant meal that is commonly served at weddings, celebrations, and special occasions.

  • Maqlooba

    Maqlooba, also spelled as "maqluba" or "makloubeh," is a traditional Middle Eastern dish that is particularly popular in countries like Palestine, Jordan, Lebanon, and Syria. The name "maqlooba" translates to "upside down" in Arabic, which is a reference to the way the dish is typically presented when served. Maqlooba is a flavorful one-pot dish that is made using a combination of rice, meat (usually chicken, lamb, or beef), and a variety of vegetables.

  • Knafeh

    Knafeh, also spelled as "kunafa," "knafeh," or "kunafeh," is a popular Middle Eastern dessert that is enjoyed in various forms across the region. It's known for its delicious combination of crispy and sweet layers, typically made from shredded or thin strands of pastry, filled with a sweet cheese or semolina mixture, and often soaked in a fragrant sugar syrup.
